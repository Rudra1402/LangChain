3.2 Margin and leverage in forex

Margin and leverage are fundamental concepts that allow traders to control larger positions with relatively small capital.

Margin is the amount of money required in your account to open and maintain a position. It functions as a good-faith deposit or collateral. For example, a 2% margin requirement means you need to deposit $2,000 to control a $100,000 position.

Leverage is the ratio between the total position size and the margin required. For instance, 50:1 leverage means you can control $50 worth of currency for every $1 in your account. Common leverage ratios include:

50:1 (2% margin)
100:1 (1% margin)
200:1 (0.5% margin)
While leverage can magnify profits, it equally magnifies losses. If a position moves against you, losses can accumulate quickly and may exceed your initial deposit if your broker doesnâ€™t offer negative balance protection
